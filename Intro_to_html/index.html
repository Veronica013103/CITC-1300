What are Crystals?

Crystals are a natural forming solid materials. The contents of a crystals consist of materials such as atoms, molecules, or ions arranged in a highly ordered micropscopic structure, forming a crystal lattice. A lattice-like structure refers to an arrangement where elements are interconnected in a repeating grid. There are seven crystal structures including cubic, monoclinic, trigonal, tetragonal, triclinic, hexagonal, and orthorhombic

How Do Crystals Form?

Crystals grow when alike molecules get close and stick together. This forms a chemical bond that is similar to velcro. Crystals require special conditions to start the growing process such as the right temperatures, molecules and a nucleation site. A nucleation site is crutial for cystal formation. This is where the crystal formation process begins. This is when the inital cluster of atoms/molecules start to form. Once a nucleation site is established, it is then allowing the crystal to grow further by adding more molecules to the existing nucleous.
